Scarlet Roadmap

This roadmap outlines Scarlet’s long‑horizon research trajectory — from raw data accumulation to advanced sentiment‑event inference. Each phase builds on the stability, interpretability, and narratability that define Scarlet’s architecture.

1. Accumulate Market Data for Offline Training (≈ 4 more Months)

Scarlet requires a deep, multi‑regime historical dataset to train a stable, interpretable forecasting model.

Goals

Collect multi‑asset OHLCV data across diverse market conditions.

Maintain schema consistency and provenance tracking.

Build a clean, corruption‑safe offline dataset.

Enable long‑horizon training cycles without drift.

2. Verify Ability to Trade Profitably

Scarlet must demonstrate that her forecasting engine can support profitable, posture‑safe trading.

Goals

Validate that Scarlet can pay for her own continued development.

Confirm that volatility‑aware posture logic behaves safely.

Evaluate suppression logic, cooldowns, and confidence scaling.

Ensure narratable reasoning aligns with actual trade outcomes.

3. Analyze Prediction Accuracy Over Time

As Scarlet’s dataset grows, her forecasting accuracy and stability should improve.

Goals

Track accuracy across rolling windows.

Compare early‑dataset vs. late‑dataset performance.

Identify regime‑specific strengths and weaknesses.

Validate that Scarlet’s emotional‑memory slopes correlate with prediction quality.

4. Branch Scarlet for Feature Expansion

If Scarlet succeeds in Steps 1–3, development splits into two synchronized branches:

Branch A — Trading Scarlet

Continues running the proven trading model.

Maintains stable, profitable posture logic.

Receives only safety and reliability updates.

Branch B — Research Scarlet

Accepts expanded feature inputs.

Integrates richer sentiment, social, and structural signals.

Experiments with new forecasting architectures.

Vision

If sentiment influences crypto, and crypto can be predicted, then sentiment can be reverse‑engineered from price behavior.

This enables Scarlet to detect major sentiment shifts before they appear in social data — a powerful early‑warning system for major events.

5. Predict the Type of Event Behind Sentiment Shifts

Once Scarlet can detect sentiment shocks, the next step is understanding their origin.

Goals

Expand Scarlet’s automated dataset pipeline.

Integrate structured and unstructured external signals.

Build classifiers that map predicted sentiment shifts to likely event categories.

Examples

Regulatory announcements

Exchange outages

Security breaches

Macro‑economic shocks

Social‑media‑driven cascades

Scarlet evolves from a market forecaster into an event‑anticipation engine.

6. Future Milestones (To Be Explored)

Scarlet’s long‑term evolution may include:

6.1 Multi‑Agent Coordination

Multiple Scarlet instances specializing in different assets.

Cross‑asset reasoning and shared emotional memory.

6.2 Real‑Time Event Narration

Scarlet explains not only what she predicts, but why.

Natural‑language summaries of expected market behavior.

6.3 Autonomous Research Cycles

Scarlet proposes new features.

Scarlet evaluates her own performance.

Scarlet schedules her own retraining cycles.

6.4 Market‑Structure Awareness

Order‑flow features.

Liquidity regime detection.

Microstructure‑aware volatility modeling.

6.5 Long‑Horizon Macro Forecasting

Detecting structural shifts in crypto ecosystems.

Predicting multi‑month sentiment cycles.

Closing Vision

Scarlet’s roadmap is designed to evolve her from:

A narratable trading agent → a predictive research engine → an event‑anticipation intelligence.

Each step builds on the last, ensuring Scarlet remains interpretable, stable, and aligned with her core design philosophy: clarity, safety, and insight.
